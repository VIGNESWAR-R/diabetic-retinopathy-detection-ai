What is Diabetic Retinopathy?

Diabetic retinopathy is a serious eye condition caused by high blood sugar levels damaging the retinaâ€™s blood vessels. If left untreated, it can lead to blindness.

Symptoms

  • Blurred or fluctuating vision
  • Dark spots or floaters in vision
  • Difficulty seeing at night
  • Loss of vision in some areas
  • Color vision impairment

Causes

Prolonged high blood sugar weakens retinal blood vessels, leading to complications. Risk factors include:

  • Poorly controlled diabetes
  • High blood pressure
  • High cholesterol
  • Smoking
  • Genetic predisposition
